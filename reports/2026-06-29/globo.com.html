Context

globo.com is the web home of Grupo Globo, the Marinho family's media conglomerate and Brazil's dominant broadcaster. The host www.globo.com is essentially a curated front door; the actual corpus lives on topic subdomains that are separate products: G1 (news), ge (sport), gshow (entertainment), and Globoplay (the subscription streaming service). The business is the classic Brazilian media triangle: broadcast and digital advertising (about two-thirds of revenue), content licensing, and a fast-growing streaming subscription line. For an index builder the interesting fact is the federated structure: each vertical publishes its own robots policy, its own dated sitemap tree, and its own ID space, so "indexing globo.com" really means indexing a dozen newsroom and video systems that happen to share a CDN (glbimg.com) and an auth wall. Public crawl is wide open on the news side and gated on the streaming side. inferred Globo treats AI-crawler policy as a per-product decision, not a corporate one. Basis: robots.txt blocks GPTBot/CCBot/Google-Extended on www, ge and gshow but those blocks are absent from g1 and globoplay (Probe Log).

Retrieval Notes (site-specific deltas)

Only where globo.com differs from the default platform assumptions.

Identifier & dedup

No single site ID space. News articles are keyed by the .ghtml URL (/{section}/…/{YYYY}/{MM}/{DD}/{slug}.ghtml); videos by a numeric media ID embedded in both the watch URL and the CDN path (e.g. 14662314). The same story can surface under G1, ge and gshow with different URLs — dedup across verticals on slug + publish date.

Retrieval unit

The article (.ghtml) for news; the video object (media ID) for clips. The portal homepage is not a retrieval unit — it is a 2.3MB editorial layout with max-age=10, meant to be re-rendered, not indexed.

Cross-modal joins

Article ↔ image joins via the <image:image> entries in the same sitemap row. Video ↔ thumbnail/stream joins via the shared numeric media ID across pox.globo.com metadata and s0*.video.glbimg.com assets. There is no published key linking an article to its source TV broadcast.

Authoritative content field

Open Graph tags, not JSON-LD, are the reliable metadata: the sampled article had no NewsArticle schema, only articleSection. Trust og:title / og:description / og:url over any inline microdata.

Freshness & recrawl

The daily-partitioned sitemap is a gift: poll only today's and yesterday's child sitemap per vertical to catch all new and updated articles, instead of recrawling the tree. lastmod is precise to the second.

Quality & spam

Low spam surface — this is first-party editorial, not UGC. The manipulation risk is the opposite: an index must respect that premium video and most of Globoplay are deliberately walled, and that "free" stream URLs in the sitemap are a curated subset, not the catalog.

Internal vs external search

On-site search (/busca/) is robots-blocked on every vertical, so external site:globo.com queries do the discovery work Globo declines to expose. The sitemap, not search, is the intended machine entry point.

User query profile

Portuguese-language, Brazil-centric: breaking news, futebol scores and tables, novela/celebrity, and "ao vivo" (live) intents. Locale (pt_BR) and regional editions (per-state G1 paths like /sp/, /ba/) are first-class facets.

Indexing Plan

  • Retrieval unit: the .ghtml article for news/sport/entertainment; the numeric-media-ID video object for clips. Treat each vertical (G1, ge, gshow) as a separate corpus sharing a CDN.
  • Primary keys & joins: article = canonical .ghtml URL (section + date + slug); video = media ID; image = signed glbimg.com source path. Join article→images within the sitemap row; join video→thumbnail/stream by media ID.
  • Ingestion paths, ranked: 1) Per-vertical dated sitemaps — the only complete, lastmod-stamped index; poll the newest child files for incrementals. 2) Article HTML for body text + OG metadata (JSON-LD is too thin to rely on). 3) Globoplay video sitemap for free-clip metadata. 4) Premium catalog — no path; design around the wall.
  • Recrawl cadence: news verticals — pull today's + yesterday's daily sitemap hourly; section/EPG pages daily; deep back catalog (pre-2024) once, then never (stable).
  • Derivatives to generate: ASR transcripts for free video clips, full-text body extraction from .ghtml, cross-vertical story dedup embeddings, and a normalized regional/section facet from the URL taxonomy.
  • Demo to build: a "daily-sitemap delta crawler" — given a vertical, fetch its dated sitemap index, diff the two newest children, and emit JSONL index docs (URL, OG fields, image set, lastmod) — proving the recrawl cadence claim above.

Risks to the Index

  • AI-crawler policy is per-product and could converge to the strict stance. G1 currently omits the AI block that www/ge/gshow already carry; if Globo standardizes robots, the largest news corpus closes overnight (Probe Log).
  • No licensing program for editorial. Crawl access ≠ reuse rights; an AI-retrieval product indexing G1 has no published license and faces a litigious rights-holder. Plan for snippet+link only.
  • inferred Free-clip stream URLs are not a stable bulk video path. Basis: content_loc appears only for a curated subset (regional editions) in the video sitemap while the premium catalog has no crawl entry; treat exposed streams as revocable.
  • Signed image CDN can rotate. glbimg.com transform URLs embed a signature; if the signing scheme changes, stored image URLs break — store the source path + transform params, not the full signed URL.
