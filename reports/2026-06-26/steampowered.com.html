Context

Steam is the dominant store and distribution platform for PC games, run by privately-held Valve and launched in 2003. Valve takes a revenue share on every sale (30% standard, falling to 25% and 20% as a title's lifetime sales pass $10M and $50M) plus in-game-transaction fees, and sells hardware (Steam Deck, Index). For an index builder, the object of interest is the app — Steam's universal appid — and the bundle of store metadata, screenshots, trailers, age ratings, and user reviews hanging off it. What makes Steam unusual among top platforms is openness: the store serves nearly all of that through stable, unauthenticated JSON endpoints (appdetails, appreviews, the search "infinite" feed), and its robots.txt blocks neither AI crawlers nor general agents (see Probe Log). The richer Steamworks Web API on api.steampowered.com needs a free key; the storefront JSON needs nothing at all. inferred Steam treats third-party indexing as a feature, not a leak. Basis: the storefront JSON serves full metadata and review bodies with no auth, and robots.txt carries no AI-crawler or sitemap restrictions (Probe Log) — the posture that lets SteamDB and SteamSpy exist.

Retrieval Notes (site-specific deltas)

Only where Steam differs from the default platform assumptions.

Identifier & dedup

The appid (a stable integer) is the universal key, present in every URL as /app/{appid}/; the trailing name slug is cosmetic and ignored on resolve. DLC, demos, soundtracks, and tools live in the same appid space as separate ids; packages and bundles are a distinct id space (subid/bundleid). One appid yields many region/language variant pages via cc/l.

Retrieval unit

Three units: the app (store page) for search and discovery, the review for opinion mining, and the package/sub for price and availability. Collapsing them loses either freshness (price) or signal (reviews).

Cross-modal joins

Everything joins on appid: screenshots, trailers, reviews, ratings, the community hub. The user-side join is appid ↔ review-author steamid, both stable integers returned directly by the JSON.

Authoritative content field

Store text is publisher-supplied marketing, not neutral. The trust signal is the aggregate review_score band ("Overwhelmingly Positive" etc.) plus the review corpus; content_descriptors and the five ratings boards are authoritative for safety/age.

Freshness & recrawl

Two regimes: price/discount churns on the sale calendar (daily during seasonal sales), while catalog metadata and media are near-static. Review counts append continuously. Recrawl price separately from everything else.

Quality & spam

Steam-specific noise: review bombing (coordinated negative campaigns), and asset-flip shovelware — roughly half of 2025's ~19K releases drew under 10 reviews. The review_score band and review volume are the practical noise filter.

Internal vs external search

On-site tag/genre search is strong, but Steam intentionally exposes JSON, so external indexes (SteamDB, SteamSpy) are first-class rather than adversarial — the opposite of the closing-wall posture seen on Amazon or X.

User query profile

Title lookup, genre/tag browse, "is it good / how long / Deck-verified", and price/deal queries. High purchase and comparison intent; little conversational traffic.

Indexing Plan

  • Retrieval unit: app = appid; reviews and packages attached as sub-records.
  • Primary keys & joins: appid primary; subid/bundleid for packages; steamid for review authors; all media join on appid.
  • Ingestion paths, ranked: 1) appdetails storefront JSON — no auth, full metadata + media URLs, soft-limited ~200 req/5 min. 2) appreviews JSON — no auth, cursor-paginated full bodies. 3) Web API GetAppList/StoreItem — free key, authoritative appid enumeration and the only complete catalog list. 4) HTML crawl of /app/ (robots-open) for fields absent from JSON.
  • Recrawl cadence: prices daily (sale-driven); review deltas weekly via cursor; catalog metadata monthly; new-release discovery via GetAppList diff.
  • Derivatives to generate: review aspect/sentiment extraction, screenshot/trailer embeddings, tag normalization, per-app price-history series.
  • Demo to build: a rate-aware appid indexer — enumerate via GetAppList, hydrate each app with appdetails + appreviews inside the ~200/5min budget, and emit appid-keyed JSONL with media URLs and a review summary (mirrors the Amazon quota-aware indexer demo).

Risks to the Index

  • The open JSON is a courtesy, not a contract: the storefront endpoints are undocumented and unversioned. Valve can tighten the soft rate limit or change shape without notice — the keyed Web API is the only surface with stability guarantees.
  • Region variance is structural: the edge geolocated this probe to CN and served Steam-China CDN URLs and CN pricing (Probe Log). An index that does not pin cc/l will silently mix price and availability across regions.
  • Review bodies sit under the Subscriber Agreement: opinion mining is fine internally, but republishing review text has no clear license — design for derived signals, not verbatim redistribution.
  • inferred The open-crawl stance could close. Basis: robots.txt today carries no AI-crawler rule and no sitemap (observed 2026-06-26), but that is a current snapshot of an editable file, not a commitment — re-check before relying on crawl access.

Inferences

  • Steam treats third-party indexing as a feature. Basis: unauthenticated full metadata and review bodies plus a robots.txt with no AI-crawler or sitemap restriction (Probe Log) — the conditions SteamDB/SteamSpy depend on.
  • appid is the universal join key. Basis: appdetails returns steam_appid, every store URL is /app/{appid}/, and screenshots, movies, and reviews are all addressed by it (observed in probe).
  • The storefront JSON is best-effort, not a contract. Basis: it carries no version path, is absent from Steamworks docs, and returned intermittent empty responses under rapid calls (rate-limit behavior, Probe Log).
  • The corpus is region-variant. Basis: the edge set steamCountry=CN and served og:image via eccdnx.com (Steam China CDN), so pricing and availability vary by cc.
