Context

yahoo.co.jp is the front door of Japan's most-used web portal, operated by LY Corporation (TSE: 4689) — the entity formed on Oct 1 2023 by merging Z Holdings, LINE, and Yahoo Japan, and controlled (62.5% of voting rights, Mar 2025) by A Holdings, a 50/50 SoftBank–Naver joint venture. Launched April 1996 as a licensed Japanese Yahoo, it long outlived US Yahoo as a habit-forming home page bundling news, mail, search, shopping, weather, and finance. The money is advertising plus commerce/PayPay, not the portal page itself. For an index builder the www host is thin — a personalized, JS-driven launcher with no sitemap and no JSON-LD — while the dense corpus lives on news.yahoo.co.jp: thousands of articles per day syndicated from ~700 media partners, richly marked up with schema.org NewsArticle. The defining trait is a split crawl policy. inferred Yahoo treats the portal as open to general search but the news corpus as off-limits to AI training/answer engines. Basis: the www robots.txt blocks only Google-Extended and GPTBot, whereas the news robots.txt Disallow: /s ~30 named AI agents (ClaudeBot, CCBot, PerplexityBot, Bytespider, Applebot-Extended…) while still admitting Googlebot/Bingbot (Probe Log).

Retrieval Notes (site-specific deltas)

Only where yahoo.co.jp differs from the default platform assumptions.

Identifier & dedup

Two ID regimes: editor-curated topics at /pickup/{numeric} (e.g. 6584653) and source articles at /articles/{40-char hex}. A pickup is a Yahoo-written digest that points at one or more source articles — dedup must treat pickup↔article as a curation edge, not a duplicate.

Retrieval unit

The source article (hex ID) is the unit for substance; the pickup is the unit for "what is Japan reading now." The portal page is not a retrieval unit — it is a personalized launcher with no durable content.

Cross-modal joins

Clean on the news side: JSON-LD joins headline ↔ image (ImageObject) ↔ publisher/author (Organization) ↔ dates per article. The weak link is article ↔ original publisher URL — Yahoo hosts a syndicated copy, so the canonical may differ from the partner's own site.

Authoritative content field

JSON-LD headline/datePublished are authoritative and clean (wire-service discipline, little SEO stuffing). The publisher is always "Yahoo!ニュース"; the real source is in author.name — do not mistake the host for the originator.

Freshness & recrawl

This is the defining delta: news articles are deliberately ephemeral, removed after a contractual retention window, so the corpus is a sliding window, not an archive. A crawler that recrawls weekly will miss most of what was published; capture must be near-real-time.

Quality & spam

Article text is curated/syndicated (low spam). The manipulation surface is the comment system — Japan's largest — where coordinated and abusive comments concentrate; comment paths are robots-blocked anyway.

Internal vs external search

The portal funnels to search.yahoo.co.jp (Google-backend web search) for the open web and to vertical search for news/shopping. On-site news search exists but its results pages are not a bulk path.

User query profile

Navigational and news-seeking in Japanese: home-page habit, "今日のニュース", finance/weather lookups, shopping. Intent skews to fresh news and transactions, not deep archival retrieval.

Indexing Plan

  • Retrieval unit: source news article (40-char hex ID) for substance; pickup (numeric ID) for trend/topic intent. Skip the portal page.
  • Primary keys & joins: article hex ID; pickup numeric ID; media-source key (from mediaList / per-source sitemap filename); join article→source via author.name and the sitemap it came from.
  • Ingestion paths, ranked: 1) news.yahoo.co.jp sitemaps (article.xml → 713 source children) with JSON-LD per page — complete and cheap for general crawlers, but only if you are not an AI-agent UA; the robots policy forbids AI crawlers, so this path is licensing-blocked for an AI index. 2) Original publisher sites for the same stories (escape the syndication wall, get archival permanence). 3) Official partner/licensing feed — the only contractually clean route for an answer engine. 4) Comments/video — no legitimate bulk path.
  • Recrawl cadence: news near-real-time (minutes–hours) because of deletion; pickup list hourly; media-source list weekly; portal not crawled.
  • Derivatives to generate: Japanese tokenization/segmentation, article→source dedup against partners' own domains, pickup→article curation graph, entity extraction (people/orgs/places) for Japanese news.
  • Demo to build: a sliding-window news capture probe — poll the 713 source sitemaps every N hours, log first-seen and last-seen lastmod per article, and measure the actual retention half-life (closes the Open Question on article lifetime), with a UA toggle showing the AI-crawler robots block.

Risks to the Index

  • AI-crawler ban on the corpus: the news robots.txt Disallow: /s ~30 AI agents (Probe Log). Indexing news.yahoo.co.jp for an AI/answer product is not permitted by their stated policy — the only safe routes are general-search display or a licensed feed.
  • Ephemerality is structural: articles are deleted on a retention schedule, so any index built from a single crawl decays to dead links. This is a content-strategy choice, not an outage.
  • Syndication, not origination: Yahoo hosts copies under partner contracts; the durable, reusable rights sit with the original media outlets, whose own sites are the more stable source.
  • inferred Split crawl policy can tighten further. Basis: the portal already blocks Google-Extended + GPTBot while news blocks ~30 agents; the trajectory is toward more AI exclusions, and a future change could extend the news-style block to the portal and verticals.
  • Ownership overhang: A Holdings (SoftBank/Naver 50/50) control and ongoing Naver-stake politics (post-2024 buyback) could shift data-sharing and access terms; treat any partner-feed deal as governance-dependent.
