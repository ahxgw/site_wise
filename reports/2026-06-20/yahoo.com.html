Context

Yahoo.com is the front door of Yahoo Inc., a privately held media-and-tech group owned by funds managed by Apollo Global Management since September 2021 (Verizon kept 10%). The portal bundles News, Finance, Sports, Mail, and a search box whose results are supplied by Microsoft Bing. Money comes from advertising (Yahoo Ads/DSP), Mail and Finance Plus subscriptions, and a search-revenue share with Microsoft. For an index builder the defining fact is that Yahoo is an aggregator, not an originator: most article pages carry a JSON-LD provider field naming the upstream publisher (a local TV station, AFP, a partner site) whose content Yahoo licenses and re-hosts on s.yimg.com / media.zenfs.com. inferred Yahoo cannot grant reuse rights to most of its own article corpus. Basis: the structured-data sample (Probe Log) shows NewsArticle.provider = "WPXI" distinct from publisher = "Yahoo News"; the licensing party for that body text is the provider, not Yahoo. The retrieval target is therefore the article-with-provenance, plus the high-value structured layer under Yahoo Finance.

Retrieval Notes (site-specific deltas)

Only where Yahoo differs from the default platform assumptions.

Identifier & dedup

Article URLs end in a numeric id (…-124055643.html) that is Yahoo's stable key. But the same wire story appears under Yahoo, the provider's own site, and other ZenFS subscribers — dedup must cluster on provider + headline + body hash, not on URL. The provider field is the cross-site anchor.

Retrieval unit

Two units: the article (with provenance) for News/Sports, and the ticker/quote record for Finance. They share almost nothing — different keys, freshness, and licensing — and should be separate indexes.

Cross-modal joins

Article JSON-LD joins text↔images (ImageObject), text↔author (Person), and text↔upstream-source (provider). Finance joins on the exchange ticker symbol, which links quotes, charts, filings, and any article that tags the symbol.

Authoritative content field

Trust the provider over the publisher: "Yahoo News" is the re-host; the provider (WPXI, AFP, …) is the editorial source of record. For canonical body text, prefer the provider's own URL when it exists.

Freshness & recrawl

News sitemap head re-stamps within the hour (Verified Metrics); Finance quotes churn by the second. Everything else (older articles) is effectively immutable until it rolls off the 902-day window — recrawl those once, then only to detect deletion.

Quality & spam

Less UGC-spam exposure than social platforms (content is editorially syndicated), but high duplication: the same agency story repeated across the portal and the sibling brands. The spam surface that matters is the comment threads, not the articles.

Internal vs external search

Yahoo's own search box is Bing's index, not Yahoo's content — so on-site "search" doesn't expose a Yahoo corpus API. To find Yahoo articles, an external index relies on the sitemaps, exactly as Google does.

User query profile

Portal traffic skews to finance lookups (ticker, "AAPL stock"), breaking-news headlines, and sports scores — short, entity-anchored, freshness-sensitive queries that reward the structured Finance layer over the article text.

Indexing Plan

  • Retrieval unit: two indexes — (a) news/sports article keyed by numeric URL id and carrying its provider; (b) Finance entity keyed by exchange ticker.
  • Primary keys & joins: article id + provider + body-hash (for cross-site dedup); ticker symbol for the finance graph; author Person and keywords[] as secondary facets.
  • Ingestion paths, ranked: 1) daily sitemap-index → ~1,475 article URLs/day, generic crawl permitted; 2) per-article JSON-LD for clean metadata + provenance; 3) news-sitemap-index for sub-hour breaking-news refresh; 4) Finance JSON endpoints for quotes — unofficial and ToS-restricted, treat as best-effort. No RSS/oEmbed/llms.txt (all blocked/absent, Probe Log).
  • Recrawl cadence: news head every 15–30 min; Finance quotes intraday; back-catalog articles once, then deletion-checks; full sitemap-index daily to catch the new day's child.
  • Derivatives to generate: body-hash for cross-publisher dedup, provider→license map, ticker NER over article text to link news to the finance graph, image embeddings only after upstream rights clearance.
  • Demo to build: a "provenance splitter" — ingest a day's sitemap, parse each article's JSON-LD, and bucket URLs by provider to show what share of yahoo.com is original vs. syndicated and which licensors dominate the corpus.

Risks to the Index

  • Licensing is upstream, not Yahoo's: body text and imagery belong to the named provider. An index that treats yahoo.com as the rights-holder is exposed to the agencies and partners it actually licenses from.
  • inferred Article text is being walled off from AI even where navigation is open. Basis: robots.txt blanket-bans ~45 AI/scraper UAs and limits Claude-SearchBot/OAI-SearchBot to Disallow: */articles/ (Probe Log) — the snippet is allowed, the body is not.
  • Search is Microsoft's, not Yahoo's: the search box rides the Bing alliance; a change in that partnership would alter what "Yahoo Search" even returns, independent of Yahoo's own content.
  • Rolling archive deletes silently: the sitemap window is ~902 days — older articles drop out of discovery without a tombstone, so an index must record its own first-seen/last-seen rather than trust the sitemap.
  • Finance endpoints are undocumented: the query*.finance.yahoo.com JSON paths have no support contract and have broken before (YQL retirement); the structured layer can vanish without notice.
