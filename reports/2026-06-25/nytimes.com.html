Context

The New York Times Company (NYSE: NYT) runs the largest subscription news business in the West: 13.08M total subscribers as of Q1 2026, on a stated path to 15M by end-2027. The product is a bundle — core news plus Cooking, The Athletic, Wirecutter, Games (Wordle/Crossword), and Audio — sold behind a metered paywall. For an index builder the object is the article, and the corpus runs back to the paper's first issue in September 1851. NYT treats that text as a licensable asset, not a crawlable one: its robots.txt opens with a legal notice reserving text-and-data-mining rights under EU DSM Art. 4 and blocks ~50 bots, including every major AI crawler (Probe Log). It is simultaneously suing OpenAI/Microsoft and selling training rights to Amazon ($20–25M/yr, May 2025). inferred NYT splits its corpus into free metadata and paid full text. Basis: the Archive API returns abstract, headline, byline, keywords and multimedia for every article since 1851 but not the body (see Verified Metrics / Access Matrix), while the robots legal header forbids TDM and Amazon paid for training access.

Retrieval Notes (site-specific deltas)

Only where NYT differs from the default platform assumptions.

Identifier & dedup

Core articles use a dated canonical path /YYYY/MM/DD/section/slug.html plus an API _id and web_url. The Athletic breaks the pattern with opaque short IDs (/athletic/…/Z8wkoK02ohtT/). Dedup the /espanol mirror, AMP variants, and syndication copies against the canonical.

Retrieval unit

The article splits into two units: a free metadata doc (API: abstract, headline, keywords, multimedia) and a gated body. Recipes, Wirecutter product reviews, and games are distinct units with their own schemas and no metadata API.

Cross-modal joins

web_url / _id join metadata, multimedia and keywords; byline → author; section_name/news_desk → desk. TimesTags entity IDs are stable join keys across articles and into the Best-Sellers and Most Popular sets.

Authoritative content field

The API abstract/lead_paragraph is editor-written and reliable. Headlines have print-vs-web variants (a headline.main plus kicker), so the headline is the SEO-distorted field; the body — the true authority — sits behind the paywall.

Freshness & recrawl

Three regimes: Top Stories / news sitemap update in minutes; archive months are immutable after a settling period (corrections append); The Athletic live blogs mutate continuously. One cadence across all three wastes quota.

Quality & spam

Editorial control makes spam a non-issue; the real hazard is staleness — free metadata can lag paywalled corrections and retractions, so an external index can surface superseded claims.

Internal vs external search

Googlebot is grouped with * and served article pages, so Google indexes NYT; AI crawlers are blocked outright. The gap between "indexed by a search engine" and "licensed for AI" is explicit here, not incidental.

User query profile

News lookups and breaking events, recipe search ("best lasagna"), sports team/player queries (Athletic), product recommendations ("best X" → Wirecutter), and games. High navigational and recency intent.

Indexing Plan

  • Retrieval unit: article metadata doc keyed by web_url/_id; recipe, product-review, and game as sibling units; full body only for licensed corpora.
  • Primary keys & joins: canonical web_url + _id; TimesTags entity IDs; byline author; section_name/news_desk.
  • Ingestion paths, ranked: 1) Archive API — month-by-month backfill of the entire 1851→now metadata corpus at 2,000 req/day (~one call per month, so a full backfill is ~2,100 calls). 2) Article Search + Top Stories + RSS for incremental fresh metadata. 3) Books / TimesTags / Most Popular for structured side-tables. 4) Full text — no legitimate bulk path; route to NYT Licensing or design metadata-only. 5) Public crawl — DataDome-walled and AI-UA-banned (Probe Log), not viable.
  • Recrawl cadence: Top Stories / news sitemap every few minutes; re-pull the trailing two Archive months periodically for corrections; older months once.
  • Derivatives to generate: entity linking from TimesTags, embeddings over abstracts (not bodies — license), image thumbnails from multimedia renditions.
  • Demo to build: an Archive-API backfiller that walks 1851→2026 within the 2,000/day budget, emits JSONL metadata docs, and explicitly flags that body text is absent by license — the metadata-vs-body split made tangible.

Risks to the Index

  • Licensing-gated, not crawl-gated: full text needs a paid contract, and AI training needs a signed deal (the Amazon precedent). Indexing bodies without one invites the OpenAI-style suit NYT is already litigating.
  • Explicit TDM reservation: the robots legal header invokes EU DSM Art. 4 opt-out and bans AI/LLM development use (Probe Log) — a documented legal signal, not just a courtesy directive.
  • Free tier is metadata-only and rate-capped: the entire AI-relevant value (body text) is excluded from the API by design; if NYT meters or paywalls the Developer APIs, the metadata backfill window also closes.
  • Bot wall hardens: DataDome challenges plus ~50 banned UAs already make public crawl non-viable; expect tightening, not loosening.
  • Uneven bundle coverage: Cooking, Games, video and comments have no metadata API; an index of "NYT" is really an index of the news/API surface plus sitemap-only islands.
