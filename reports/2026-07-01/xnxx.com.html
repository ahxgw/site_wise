Context

xnxx.com is one of the largest adult video tubes — a free, ad-funded site mixing uploader UGC with studio catalogs. It is owned by WGCZ Holding (WebGroup Czech Republic, Prague), which also runs the near-identical sister site xvideos.com. For an index builder it is the structural opposite of Netflix (yesterday's report): robots.txt disallows only two paths, every video page ships a complete schema.org VideoObject, and the JSON-LD carries a directly fetchable — though time-limited, signed — MP4 contentUrl, so the media itself, not just metadata, is technically retrievable. The subtle twist is that xnxx runs the same codebase and the same CDN (CDN77 under *.xnxx-cdn.com) as xvideos, so the two sites are largely one corpus mirrored twice. inferred The model is purely ad-funded with a premium upsell, no content paywall. Basis: video pages serve full JSON-LD and fetchable media without login, while the homepage links out to the xnxx.gold premium domain (Probe Log). The binding constraint is law, not technology: EU DSA VLOP duties, mandated age verification, and copyright/CSAM liability.

Retrieval Notes (site-specific deltas)

Only where xnxx differs from the default platform assumptions (and from its xvideos.com sibling).

Identifier & dedup

The video ID is the short base36 token in /video-{id}/slug (slug decorative). But the real dedup key is the CDN asset UUID in the contentUrl/thumbnailUrl path: the same media appears on both xnxx and xvideos, so keying on the site video ID double-counts one corpus.

Retrieval unit

The video. Uploader/channel and pornstar are secondary entities that group videos; the tag set carries the topical signal.

Cross-modal joins

Video ID ↔ uploader_id ↔ thumbnail/MP4 CDN UUID. The CDN UUID is the join that spans sister sites — it, not the per-site ID, links xnxx and xvideos records to the same asset.

Authoritative content field

Unlike Netflix's JS shell, the JSON-LD here is complete and machine-clean on first fetch. Title/description are SEO keyword strings; trust the tags and structured fields over the title.

Freshness & recrawl

Poll sitemap_uploads_new*.xml — it is the incremental feed. Crucially, media contentUrls carry an expiring signed token, so they must be re-resolved at fetch time; a cached media URL rots within hours.

Quality & spam

Adult-specific: tag spam, title keyword-stuffing, and heavy re-upload/duplication (including across the sister site). The dominant risk is legal (copyright/CSAM), not ranking spam.

Internal vs external search

On-site search plus tag/category browse; because robots is open, external search engines index the video pages directly — unlike Netflix, there is no closed-search wall.

User query profile

Category/tag and performer queries, largely navigational. Retrieval intents are attribute- and entity-constrained rather than conversational.

Indexing Plan

  • Retrieval unit: the video, keyed by its /video-{id} token; uploader, tags, thumbnail and view count attach to it.
  • Primary keys & joins: site video ID as the local key; dedup across the xvideos sister site on the CDN asset UUID embedded in contentUrl/thumbnailUrl.
  • Ingestion paths, ranked: 1) sitemap_uploads_new*.xml (~23.3K recent URLs) → crawl each for JSON-LD (robots-permissive). 2) sitemap_main.xml for the entity graph (categories, tags, channels, pornstars). 3) DSA Article 40 researcher data access as a potential vetted bulk path (a VLOP obligation). 4) Media: signed MP4/HLS is fetchable at crawl time but tokens expire — no persistent bulk-mirror license.
  • Recrawl cadence: poll the uploads sitemaps frequently (high velocity); entity pages weekly; re-resolve every contentUrl per fetch because of the expiring token.
  • Derivatives to generate: a CDN-UUID concordance to dedup against xvideos; ASR + keyframe + NSFW/CSAM classifiers on video; tag normalization.
  • Demo to build: a cross-site dedup prober — sample both the xnxx and xvideos sitemaps, extract the CDN asset UUID from each JSON-LD contentUrl, and report the overlap rate, i.e. how much of "two sites" is actually one corpus.

Risks to the Index

  • Law, not technology, is the wall: EU DSA VLOP duties and mandated age verification can gate the crawl surface in specific jurisdictions even though robots.txt is open; copyright/CSAM liability constrains any media retention.
  • Signed, expiring media URLs: the contentUrl token is time-limited (Probe Log), so cached media links rot and must be re-fetched — an index cannot durably store the media URL.
  • inferred Cross-site duplication inflates any count. Basis: xnxx and xvideos share the CDN asset UUID space on *.xnxx-cdn.com and the same platform codebase (observed 2026-07-01); naive per-site counting double-counts one corpus.
  • Private company, no official corpus numbers: the sitemap exposes only ~23.3K recent uploads, so total catalog size and upload rate are unknowable without enumeration or a data-access grant.
