Context

Amazon.com is the largest Western online store and the center of a marketplace where third-party sellers supply 61% of units sold. The company monetizes the catalog three times over: retail margin, seller services (commissions, fulfillment, $172.2B in 2025), and advertising sold against product searches ($68.6B in 2025). For an index builder, the interesting object is not the page but the ASIN — Amazon's universal product identifier — and the graph around it: variation families, offers, sellers, brands, browse nodes, ratings. Amazon treats this graph as a paid asset: public crawl is actively defended, AI crawlers are banned outright, and the official APIs meter access in proportion to the sales you refer. inferred Amazon prices catalog access in sales rather than dollars. Basis: the PA-API quota formula grants +1 TPS per $4,320 of shipped referral revenue and revokes access after 30 days without sales (see Verified Metrics); the product-page bot wall observed in the Probe Log routes automated access to those same APIs.

Retrieval Notes (site-specific deltas)

Only where Amazon differs from the default platform assumptions.

Identifier & dedup

The ASIN is one of the strongest IDs on the web: 10 characters, stable, printed in every URL (/dp/{ASIN}). Dedup against URL aliases (/gp/product/, tracking params) and variation families — one "product" is a parent ASIN plus child ASINs per size/color. Each marketplace (.com, .co.uk, .de) is a separate ASIN namespace.

Retrieval unit

Three distinct units: the product (variation family) for search, the offer (ASIN × seller × condition) for price/availability, and the rating summary for quality signal. Collapsing them loses either freshness or relevance.

Cross-modal joins

Everything joins on ASIN: images, offers, ratings, browse nodes, brand, ads. GTIN/UPC↔ASIN mapping (PA-API ItemInfo.ExternalIds) connects Amazon's namespace to the rest of commerce.

Authoritative content field

Titles are keyword-stuffed by sellers and the least trustworthy field. Structured attributes and images are closer to ground truth; ratings carry the quality signal but are an adversarial surface (200M+ fake reviews blocked in one year, Verified Metrics).

Freshness & recrawl

Two regimes: offer data (price, stock, buy-box winner) is stale within hours; catalog metadata and images churn over weeks. An index refreshing both at one cadence wastes its quota on the wrong objects.

Quality & spam

Amazon-specific manipulation: fake reviews, listing hijacking (content swapped under an established ASIN), variation abuse to inherit reviews, counterfeit offers under legitimate ASINs. Offer provenance matters as much as the product.

Internal vs external search

Product search happens on-site. Google may crawl product pages (robots.txt allows generic agents) but its copy lags on price and stock — exactly the fields that churn fastest. External indexes of Amazon are structurally stale.

User query profile

High purchase intent: brand + model, attribute-constrained ("USB-C hub 4K 60Hz"), comparison and reorder queries. Conversational queries are shifting to Rufus, Amazon's shopping assistant, rather than the search box.

Indexing Plan

  • Retrieval unit: product = ASIN variation family; offers and rating summaries attached as fast-refresh sub-records.
  • Primary keys & joins: marketplace + ASIN; offer = ASIN + sellerId + condition; GTIN↔ASIN for cross-store joins.
  • Ingestion paths, ranked: 1) PA-API 5.0 — metadata, images, offers, browse nodes; starts at 8,640 lookups/day and scales only with referral sales. 2) SP-API — own-catalog data plus ANY_OFFER_CHANGED notifications for near-real-time price churn. 3) Public crawl — effectively closed to non-search-engine agents (Probe Log: WAF challenge + bot-wall 404). 4) Review text — no legitimate path; design around star/count only.
  • Recrawl cadence: offers via SP-API push, not polling; catalog metadata weekly; images on listing change; browse-node tree monthly.
  • Derivatives to generate: attribute normalization across sellers, image embeddings for visual dedup of hijacked/counterfeit listings, GTIN concordance table.
  • Demo to build: a quota-aware PA-API indexer — paste ASINs, fetch within TPS/TPD budget, emit JSONL index docs, and surface which fields the quota forces you to skip (mirror of the TikTok oEmbed demo).

Risks to the Index

  • Quota is revenue-coupled: PA-API access dies after 30 days without referred sales (docs); an index that doesn't sell loses its feed.
  • Review data is closing, not opening: Seller Central's Customer Reviews tool was deprecated Sep 30 2025; PA-API never carried review bodies. Plan for ratings-only permanently.
  • Bot defense is first-party infrastructure: the WAF challenge observed in the Probe Log is AWS's own product; expect it to harden faster than third-party defenses elsewhere.
  • inferred AI retrieval products face enforcement, not licensing. Basis: robots.txt blanket-bans ~30 named AI crawlers including agent-style UAs (observed 2026-06-10), and no AI licensing program is published on the developer portal.
