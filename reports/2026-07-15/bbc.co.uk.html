Context

The BBC is a UK public-service broadcaster operating under a Royal Charter (current term Jan 2017–Dec 2027), funded mainly by a £174.50/year TV license fee (~64% of FY2024/25 income) plus BBC Studios commercial revenue. bbc.co.uk serves the domestic UK audience; the sibling host bbc.com serves international readers (ad-supported, 40+ language World Service editions) — same CMS, geo-split monetization. Matt Brittin (ex-Google EMEA president) became Director-General 18 May 2026, succeeding Tim Davie. A government Charter Review launched 16 Dec 2025 is weighing funding-model changes (sliding-scale fee, advertising, subscription tiers) before the charter expires, which would directly reshape licensing terms this report assumes. For an index builder, the BBC runs an unusually sharp split: robots.txt names 35+ AI/scraping user agents and disallows them all, plus a plain-English ban on training, RAG, agentic use, and even personal-use summarization — yet the same robots.txt still publishes 14 Sitemap directives and the domain keeps open RSS and podcast feeds. The legitimate surface is real but narrow and feed-shaped, not crawl-shaped.

Retrieval Notes (site-specific deltas)

Identifier & dedup

Article URLs use an opaque ID (/news/articles/{14-char-id}), replacing the older /news/{category}-{numeric-id} scheme still visible in archive sitemap entries (Probe Log: 2010-era URLs use the old pattern) — two ID formats coexist across the archive, so canonicalization must handle both.

Retrieval unit

The article is the natural unit; the JSON-LD block is the only place multi-byline authorship (Probe Log: two Person entries, no author ID/URL) and precise dateModified are exposed together, so it should be the primary parse target over visible page text.

Cross-modal joins

No stable cross-modal ID observed: video embeds join to an article only via the article's own URL path embedding a shortcode (.../articles/{id}/p0nyhnkr/...), and podcast episodes have no linkage back to article text at all in the sampled feed.

Freshness & recrawl

The rolling news sitemap's 2-day window (Probe Log) is far shorter than a typical news sitemap's 48h-to-a-week norm elsewhere, and the archive index's own lastmod values (oldest checked: 2023-12-21) don't reflect ongoing archive growth — recrawl scheduling can't rely on index-level dates and needs per-URL lastmod from the child sitemaps instead.

Quality & spam

Low spam risk relative to marketplace/UGC sites — single-publisher editorial content; the operative risk is stated-terms non-compliance (robots.txt's AI-training/RAG ban) rather than content trustworthiness.

Internal vs external search

robots.txt disallows /search, /bitesize/search, /cbbc/search, /cbeebies/search, and /iplayer/search for all crawlers (Probe Log) — the BBC's own on-site search is explicitly excluded from any external index, pushing discovery entirely onto sitemaps/RSS/topic pages.

Indexing Plan

  • Retrieval unit: article (opaque-ID and legacy-ID variants); podcast episode as a separate unit joined only by programme feed, not by article ID.
  • Primary keys & joins: canonical article URL as primary key; JSON-LD datePublished/dateModified pair as the change-detection signal, since sitemap-level lastmod is unreliable at the archive-index layer (Probe Log).
  • Ingestion paths, ranked: 1) feeds.bbci.co.uk RSS — free, no auth, but only the current front page/topic window. 2) The 14 declared sitemaps — free, no auth, covers up to ~9M URLs combined across both hosts (upper bound, Verified Metrics), but the rolling news child only holds ~48h and the archive index's own dates are stale, so full-archive backfill needs one full sitemap walk plus incremental RSS after. 3) Podcast RSS per programme — free, standard format, but requires enumerating programme IDs first (no master podcast index found). 4) Video/audio media files themselves — no legitimate bulk path; only sitemap/embed metadata is open.
  • Recrawl cadence: RSS-driven near-real-time for front-page/breaking news; daily sitemap diff for topic/archive growth; podcast feeds on their own per-programme publish cadence.
  • Derivatives to generate: ASR transcripts for iPlayer/Sounds audio (BBC does not expose transcripts in the sampled feeds); article-to-video-embed linking via the shortcode pattern observed in URLs, since no explicit join key exists.
  • Demo to build: a robots.txt policy-vs-technical-access diff tool — fetch the sitemap/RSS surface (which remains technically open) and cross-reference it against the 35+ named Disallow entries and the plain-English AI-use ban, to make the gap between "crawlable" and "permitted" an explicit, auditable output rather than an assumption.

Risks to the Index

  • Explicit stated-terms ban on AI training, RAG, and summarization (Probe Log) is unusually specific and recently strengthened industry-wide language; treating the open sitemaps/RSS as implicit permission for those uses is the single largest legal-posture risk in this report.
  • Charter Review (launched 16 Dec 2025) may change the funding/licensing model before end-2027, with advertising, subscription, or sliding-scale options on the table — any of these could tighten (paywall) or loosen (ad-funded openness) today's Access Matrix. House of Commons Library: The future of the BBC licence fee
  • inferred The rolling news sitemap's short (~48h) window means an index that only polls sitemaps, not RSS, will silently miss same-day corrections/updates once they roll off. Basis: news sitemap index carries only 2 children at ~1,000 URLs each (Probe Log, 2026-07-15), far below the archive's 50,000-per-child scale.
  • New Director-General (Matt Brittin, since 18 May 2026) came from outside broadcasting (ex-Google) — a plausible signal of technology-strategy change during the Charter Review window, though no concrete API/access-policy change has been announced yet (Open Questions).
