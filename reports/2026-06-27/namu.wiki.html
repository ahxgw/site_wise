Context

namu.wiki is the dominant Korean-language wiki — a fandom-and-pop-culture-heavy general reference that draws roughly 7× the traffic of Korean Wikipedia (Oct 2024) and ranks among South Korea's most-visited sites. It was forked on 2015-04-17 from a 2015-04-11 snapshot of Rigveda Wiki after that site's owner privatized it, and is operated by umanle S.R.L. in Asunción, Paraguay — an offshore posture that has repeatedly frustrated Korean takedown demands. It runs on a proprietary engine, "the seed," with its own markup (NamuMark), not MediaWiki, and monetizes via top-banner ads (since Sep 2018). For an index builder, namu.wiki is a paradox: enormous, authoritative-for-Korea content under a single license, behind one of the hardest walls in this series. inferred The site treats all automated access as hostile rather than negotiating per-crawler. Basis: robots.txt carries no GPTBot/ClaudeBot-style directives (Probe Log) — a uniform Cloudflare challenge fronts every path instead, so naming AI agents is unnecessary.

Retrieval Notes (site-specific deltas)

Only where namu.wiki differs from the default platform assumptions.

Identifier & dedup

The key is the document title string (URL-encoded into /w/{title}) — Korean-language, case- and spacing-sensitive, no opaque numeric ID. Redirects (동음이의/리다이렉트) are first-class objects and inflate the 8.28M total; dedup must collapse redirect titles onto their target. Revisions carry rN numbers and per-revision UUIDs (seen in search-result URLs).

Retrieval unit

The document, but namu.wiki documents are unusually long and section-heavy (table-of-contents trees, nested folding). Paragraph- or section-level units keyed by anchor beat whole-document retrieval for this corpus.

Authoritative content field

Source is NamuMark, not wikitext or HTML — a custom syntax requiring a dedicated parser ("the seed" engine). Rendered HTML is the display truth; raw NamuMark is the editable truth. No MediaWiki API means standard wiki tooling does not apply.

Freshness & recrawl

Two regimes split hard: a frozen 2021 bulk dump versus a live surface reachable only one challenged page at a time. There is no incremental feed (no RSS/sitemap/API) to bridge them, so any fresh index must re-render the wall per page.

Quality & spam

Community-edited pop-culture content: opinionated, citation-light, and subject to edit wars on fandom/political topics. Trust varies by document maturity; the revision history (/history/) is the only built-in provenance signal.

Internal vs external search

Despite robots.txt Allowing /w/ and /Search for all agents, the Cloudflare wall means generic crawlers cannot fetch — yet pages still surface in Google (search snippets read the live count). inferred Major search engines pass the challenge via an arrangement or allowlist that ordinary crawlers do not. Basis: robots permits the paths, every probe UA got 403 (Probe Log), but indexed snippets of /w/ pages exist.

Indexing Plan

  • Retrieval unit: document section, keyed by {title}#{anchor}; whole-document and redirect records attached.
  • Primary keys & joins: document title (canonical) ← redirect titles; title → revision rN/UUID for provenance; title → backlinks and categories for the graph.
  • Ingestion paths, ranked: 1) frozen pre-2021 JSON dump (third-party mirror) — only true bulk corpus, but stale and license-restricted; 2) challenge-solving headless render of live /w/ pages — high cost per page, no bulk; 3) Google/search snippets as a thin freshness signal. No official API, no sitemap, no current dump.
  • Recrawl cadence: impossible to schedule cheaply — no incremental feed; budget per-page challenge solving for high-value documents only.
  • Derivatives to generate: a NamuMark→clean-text parser; redirect-collapse table; section embeddings; revision-based freshness scores.
  • Demo to build: a NamuMark parser + redirect-resolver over a sample of the archived dump that emits section-level JSONL index docs and flags how much of the 8.28M total is redirects vs general documents.

Risks to the Index

  • License is the hard ceiling: CC BY-NC-SA 2.0 KR is NonCommercial — a commercial search product cannot legitimately display or redistribute this corpus, dump or no dump. This blocks more than the wall does.
  • No legitimate bulk path since 2021: official dumps were discontinued; the best corpus is years stale and only via third-party mirrors of uncertain standing.
  • Uniform bot wall, no negotiation: the Cloudflare challenge fronts every path (Probe Log) and there is no API or AI-licensing program to opt into; expect access to harden, not open.
  • Offshore governance: umanle S.R.L. operates from Paraguay, outside Korean jurisdiction — content and access policy can change without the recourse a domestic operator would face.
