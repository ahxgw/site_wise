Context

bbc.com is BBC Global News Ltd's ad-supported international English edition (plus 42 World Service language editions), the commercial-and-World-Service counterpart to the license-fee-funded bbc.co.uk covered in Site Wise's 2026-07-15 report. The BBC bought the bbc.com domain in 1999 for $375,000 (price revealed only in 2005); an international "World Edition" split from the UK site in 2002. Same CMS and BBC-GTM/Belfrage/Fastly edge stack as bbc.co.uk, but a different configuration: no content-security-policy header observed, x-frame-options: SAMEORIGIN (vs DENY on bbc.co.uk), and ozone/permutive ad-tech scripts present on-page — first-hand confirmation of the ad-supported model. bbc.com also carries a lifestyle vertical branded internally as "bbcx" (Travel, Worklife, Culture, Future Planet), dominated by Travel (61% of its 435 top-level section pages). For an index builder, the site inherits bbc.co.uk's plain-English AI-training/RAG ban and 33-name bot blocklist verbatim, but its sitemap surface is messier: three per-language sitemaps sampled are frozen at a 2014 snapshot pointing at bbc.co.uk URLs, while the real current World Service surface lives in per-language RSS feeds and one shared 20K-URL topics sitemap instead.

Retrieval Notes (site-specific deltas)

Identifier & dedup

Same two article-ID eras as bbc.co.uk (legacy .shtml paths still live inside the stale per-language sitemaps vs. current opaque-ID /news/articles/{id}), plus a third scheme unique to bbc.com: the bbcx vertical uses human slugs (/travel, /worklife) for sections and PID-style IDs (p0m2k1h8) for documentary episodes (Probe Log) — three co-existing URL grammars on one domain.

Retrieval unit

Article (opaque ID) for news — same as bbc.co.uk; documentary episode (PID-style ID) as a distinct unit for the bbcx vertical, with no observed join back to article content.

Cross-modal joins

The sampled article's embedded VideoObject has no join key to the separate documentary catalog: its contentUrl is just the article's own URL (Probe Log), so article-embedded clips and the 3,013-URL documentary catalog read as two disconnected inventories, not one media library with two entry points.

Freshness & recrawl

The three sampled /{lang}/sitemap.xml files are dead (2014 snapshot, Probe Log) and must not drive recrawl scheduling; the live World Service freshness signal is feeds.bbci.co.uk/{lang}/rss.xml (confirmed current) plus sitemap-com-ws-topics.xml, neither of which carries the article-level lastmod that sitemap-com-archive.xml provides for English content.

Quality & spam

Same low-spam single-publisher profile as bbc.co.uk; the added wrinkle is stale-sitemap noise — a crawler trusting /{lang}/sitemap.xml at face value would ingest a decade-old, wrong-domain URL list instead of live content (Probe Log).

Internal vs external search

Same site-wide /search, /rd/search, /sounds/search disallow pattern as bbc.co.uk (Probe Log); bbc.com's robots.txt additionally blocks /food/search, /food/recipes/search, /education/my, and /bitesize/my.

Indexing Plan

  • Retrieval unit: article (opaque ID), same primary unit as bbc.co.uk; documentary episode (PID-style ID) as a separate unit for the bbcx vertical.
  • Primary keys & joins: canonical article URL; JSON-LD datePublished/dateModified pair as the change-detection signal; no working cross-modal key between embedded VideoObject clips and the documentary catalog (Retrieval Notes).
  • Ingestion paths, ranked: 1) feeds.bbci.co.uk/{lang}/rss.xml per World Service language — free, live, front-page window only (confirmed working for Mundo, Probe Log). 2) sitemap-com-archive.xml + sitemap-com-news.xml — free, up to ~6.05M archive URLs, but the 33-sitemap surface mixes 3+ stale per-language dumps (skip) with live vertical/archive sitemaps (use). 3) sitemap-com-ws-topics.xml — free, 20,259 topic URLs, the only current per-language discovery path for non-English World Service content since the /{lang}/sitemap.xml files are dead. 4) bbcx/video_documentaries_sitemap.xml — free, 3,013 URLs, metadata-only path to the documentary catalog; no bulk media-file access exists.
  • Recrawl cadence: RSS-driven for the ~48h English news window and each live World Service language feed; daily diff on sitemap-com-archive.xml and ws-topics.xml for backfill; bbcx index/audio/video sitemaps on a slower cadence based on observed lastmod spacing (2026-07-13 to 2026-07-17 in one week's sample, Probe Log).
  • Derivatives to generate: ASR for World Service podcasts/documentaries (no transcripts exposed in the sampled feeds); a slug-to-opaque-ID resolver for the bbcx vertical, since /travel//worklife//culture section pages use human slugs while the news CMS has moved to opaque IDs.
  • Demo to build: a sitemap-freshness classifier — fetch every Sitemap: line bbc.com's own robots.txt declares and flag which are actually current (a fresh child lastmod or a live RSS counterpart) vs. dead/vestigial like the three /{lang}/sitemap.xml files this report found frozen at 2014, turning "the sitemap exists" into "the sitemap is still true" across all 33 declared lines.

Risks to the Index

  • ODA funding cuts: ~80% of World Service government funding is sourced from UK Official Development Assistance, which the government plans to shrink to 0.3% of Gross National Income by 2027 (Verified Metrics) — further language-service closures would shrink the sitemap/RSS surface this plan relies on.
  • robots.txt's plain-English AI-training/RAG/summarization ban is already broader here (33 named bot disallows, Probe Log) than on bbc.co.uk; a future shift from stated-terms restriction to technical blocking (e.g. Cloudflare bot management) would close the sitemap/RSS surface entirely rather than just prohibiting its reuse.
  • Stale sitemaps stay declared in robots.txt: if the World Service ever repoints or deletes the dead /{lang}/sitemap.xml files without updating robots.txt (or vice versa), a pipeline built against literal Sitemap: lines would silently ingest garbage instead of failing loud.

Inferences

  • bbc.com is ad-supported while bbc.co.uk is license-fee funded. Basis: ozone/permutive ad-tech scripts observed on the sampled bbc.com article (Probe Log), plus BBC Global News Ltd's documented role as the BBC's commercial international-news subsidiary.
  • Article-embedded video clips and the bbcx documentary catalog are two disconnected inventories, not one media library. Basis: the sampled article's VideoObject.contentUrl points at the article's own URL, not a documentary catalog entry, and no shared ID field appears in either JSON-LD block (Probe Log).
  • The /{lang}/sitemap.xml files are dead weight for crawling, not a usable discovery path. Basis: all sampled entries date to Sept 2014 and link bbc.co.uk paths, while feeds.bbci.co.uk/mundo/rss.xml returned current content in the same session (Probe Log).
