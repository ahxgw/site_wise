Shared Rules

Methodology

How Site Wise reports are written: evidence tags, numeric conventions, default platform assumptions, and the probe playbook. Reports state only what is specific to the site; everything generic lives here.

Evidence Tags

  • verified Sourced to a primary or reliable secondary record, or observed first-hand in the probe log. Must link the source or name the probe.
  • inferred A reasoned interpretation. Must name its basis inline (Basis: robots.txt line N / oEmbed field X / observed header Y). An inference that cannot name a concrete signal gets deleted, not tagged.
  • open Unknown or contested. Every open item lives exactly once, in the report's Open Questions list, with the query, API, dump, or measurement that would close it.

Numeric Conventions

  • Every figure carries an as-of date and a source. A number without a date is treated as a defect.
  • Use compact units (70K, 1.2M, 20B, 1.1PB) unless the exact value is itself meaningful (IDs, quotas).
  • Mark whether a number is directly reported or calculated by Site Wise.
  • Third-party traffic estimates are not treated as official figures; they appear only with the estimator named.

Default Assumptions for Large Platforms

The following hold for essentially every major logged-in platform. Reports do not repeat them; they state only where the site deviates.

  • Logged-out / anonymous crawl does not reflect the personalized product. Region, language, age, device, and account state change results.
  • Official embeds / oEmbed are the safest public display path; bulk media mirroring requires explicit rights review.
  • robots.txt commonly blocks AI crawlers (GPTBot, ClaudeBot, PerplexityBot, Google-Extended, Applebot-Extended, etc.); the report notes only the deltas or unusual allowances.
  • The legitimately indexable surface splits into two tracks: a curated SEO sliver served only to approved search crawlers (allowlisted UAs, selective sitemaps, UA- or JS-dependent rendering), and official APIs metered by revenue, eligibility, or researcher credentials. Generic public crawl sits between the tracks and is being actively closed. Reports state which track each modality rides and what the meter charges.
  • Internal ranking is the dominant distribution channel; spam and manipulation incentives concentrate wherever ranking rewards attention.
  • Internal metadata (ranking features, safety labels, engagement telemetry) is far richer than anything exposed externally.
  • Trends decay fast; back catalogs persist. Recrawl cadence should follow object class, not site-wide averages.

Probe Playbook

Each report must include at least three first-hand probes, recorded in its Probe Log with date, command, and observation. Standard probes:

Writing Rules

  • Lede: at most 25 words. It doubles as the index-card summary.
  • Context: origin, owner, business model, and why the site matters fit in one section of at most 200 words. No section for intents, product anatomy, or lessons unless a claim there is evidenced and site-specific.
  • Access Matrix is the core deliverable: modality × objects × legitimate path × auth/quota × license/display × freshness. Every cell must be verifiable or marked open.
  • Deltas only: retrieval notes state how this site differs from the default assumptions above.
  • One home per fact: an open question appears once (Open Questions), a number appears once (Verified Metrics), a probe appears once (Probe Log). Other sections reference them instead of restating them.
  • Run python3 scripts/lint_report.py before committing; it flags untagged links, basis-less inferences, and oversized ledes.
