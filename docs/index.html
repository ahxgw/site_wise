tiktok.com 2026-06-09

TikTok is a short-video recommendation network where the primary graph is not who you know but what you watch, replay, skip, remix, save, search, buy, and share.

 instagram.com 2026-06-08

Instagram is Meta's visual social graph: a photo, video, messaging, creator, shopping, and recommendation surface where identity and entertainment collapse into one feed.

 google.com 2026-06-07

Google is the web's dominant intent router: a search box, answer engine, advertising marketplace, crawler, browser-default destination, local/navigation layer, and AI interface that turns the open web into ranked, monetized, increasingly synthesized responses.

 reddit.com 2026-06-06

Reddit is a community-organized discussion corpus where posts, comments, votes, moderation, identity, and subreddit context turn everyday human questions into one of the web's most valuable public-but-controlled retrieval surfaces.

 facebook.com 2026-06-05

Facebook is the web's largest identity-backed social graph still operating as a daily utility: a feed, groups, pages, marketplace, events, video, messaging-adjacent surface, and advertising inventory system whose most valuable corpus is not a public web archive but a permissioned, personalized, multimodal graph of people, relationships, interests, posts, reactions, groups, pages, listings, and local institutions.

 wikipedia.org 2026-06-04

Wikipedia is the web's default neutral reference layer: a multilingual, volunteer-governed encyclopedia that converts external sources into linked, reusable summaries, and now sits at the center of search, AI, knowledge graphs, education, and public-memory disputes.

 youtube.com 2026-06-03

YouTube is the world's dominant full-modal video index: more than 20 billion uploaded videos, an average of more than 20 million new videos per day, 200 billion Shorts views per day, and a creator economy whose public corpus is valuable precisely because every video binds moving image, audio, text metadata, captions, thumbnails, comments, channel identity, rights state, and recommendation behavior.
