Daily Site Profiles

A mobile-friendly archive for deep profiles of high-traffic websites.

workspace.google.com 2026-07-16

workspace.google.com is Google's subscription storefront for Gmail, Drive, Docs, and Meet: a 3B-user suite sold through IP-priced tiers, not a crawlable content corpus.

 bbc.co.uk 2026-07-15

The BBC is the UK's license-fee-funded public broadcaster: open sitemaps and RSS feeds sit behind a robots.txt that explicitly forbids AI training, RAG, and summarization.

 rakuten.co.jp 2026-07-14

Rakuten Ichiba is Japan's biggest shopping mall — merchants run storefronts stitched together by loyalty Points, catalog metered through a 1-req/sec API, not open crawl.

 support.google.com 2026-07-13

Google's shared Help Center: one domain hosting dozens of per-product article and Q&amp;A namespaces, server-rendering text but injecting structured data only via JS.

 naver.com 2026-07-12

Naver is South Korea's dominant portal and search engine (~63% share), locking news, blogs, cafes, and shopping behind explicit anti-AI-crawl robots.txt rules.

 cricbuzz.com 2026-07-11

Cricbuzz is Times Internet's cricket news, live-score, and stats portal — India's dominant cricket app, now part-owned by Dream11's parent alongside Willow TV.

 github.com 2026-07-10

GitHub is Microsoft's $7.5B-acquired code host, now home to 630M repos and 180M developers, whose canonical record lives entirely in its API, not crawlable HTML.

 rottentomatoes.com 2026-07-09

Rotten Tomatoes aggregates critic and ticket-verified audience scores for 2.3M+ film/TV titles behind an unusually open robots.txt but a JS-rendered, API-free review layer.

 canva.com 2026-07-08

Canva is a freemium drag-and-drop design platform whose public crawl surface is almost entirely blocked by a Cloudflare bot challenge, robots.txt notwithstanding.

 yandex.ru 2026-07-07

yandex.ru is Russia's dominant search portal (~72% share), but its homepage has redirected to VK-owned dzen.ru since 2022, splitting ownership across one domain.

 gemini.google.com 2026-07-06

Gemini is Google's AI assistant with 900M monthly users; the app host has no robots.txt and noindexes even its public shared conversations.

 chatgpt.com 2026-07-05

ChatGPT is OpenAI's chat product with 900M weekly users, but its own domain is a Cloudflare-gated app shell with almost no crawlable text corpus.

 ilovepdf.com 2026-07-04

iLovePDF is a top-50 document-tool site whose only indexable corpus is ~1.4K marketing pages — the files users bring are deleted within hours.

 whatsapp.com 2026-07-03

WhatsApp is a 3B-user messenger whose corpus is end-to-end encrypted and unindexable; its only public surface is a marketing site and a paid business API.

 espn.com 2026-07-02

ESPN walls its article HTML and video behind CloudFront, but leaves the entire structured sports graph open on an unauthenticated, CORS-enabled JSON API.

 xnxx.com 2026-07-01

xnxx.com is XVideos' sister tube: open crawl, complete VideoObject JSON-LD, and signed fetchable MP4s — one corpus mirrored across two huge adult sites.

 netflix.com 2026-06-30

Netflix is the largest subscription streaming service: a $45B catalog of ~25.7K titles whose public pages welcome AI crawlers yet expose no content API.

 globo.com 2026-06-29

globo.com is Brazil's largest media portal: a thin homepage fronting per-vertical subdomains (G1, ge, gshow, Globoplay) whose corpora ride dated sitemaps, not APIs.

 xhamster.com 2026-06-28

xHamster is a Cyprus-owned adult video tube — videos, photos, erotic stories, cams — that publicly disputes EU very-large-platform status to avoid heavier regulation.

 namu.wiki 2026-06-27

namu.wiki is Korea's most-visited knowledge base — 8M+ documents on a proprietary engine, wrapped in a Cloudflare challenge that blocks every bot and bulk path.

 steampowered.com 2026-06-26

Steam is Valve's PC game store — 265K products keyed by appid, exposed through unauthenticated storefront JSON endpoints and an unusually open robots.txt.

 nytimes.com 2026-06-25

The New York Times is a metered-paywall news archive to 1851: metadata flows through free APIs, full text only through paid licensing.

 xvideos.com 2026-06-24

xvideos.com is the open extreme of web video: permissive crawl, full JSON-LD, and fetchable signed MP4s — gated by law and age checks, not technology.

 open.spotify.com 2026-06-23

open.spotify.com is Spotify's web player: a 100M-track catalog graph exposed as rich page metadata and oEmbed, but gated behind OAuth and a 2024-pruned API.

 pornhub.com 2026-06-22

Pornhub is the web's largest adult-video site: a verified-uploader-only catalog of ~4M videos exposed through a free, unauthenticated public metadata API.

 x.com 2026-06-21

x.com (formerly Twitter) is a real-time public-conversation network now owned by xAI; its post firehose is metered through pay-per-use API credits, not crawling.

 yahoo.com 2026-06-20

Yahoo.com is a 30-year web portal — news, finance, sports, mail, Bing-powered search — owned by Apollo, aggregating licensed publisher content under per-article provider attribution.

 play.google.com 2026-06-19

Google Play is Android's crawlable catalog of ~1.85M apps keyed by package name — and a court is forcing Google to share it with rivals.

 yahoo.co.jp 2026-06-18

yahoo.co.jp is Japan's dominant web portal — an LY Corporation property whose deep indexable mass is the ephemeral, AI-crawler-walled news corpus on news.yahoo.co.jp.

 microsoft.com 2026-06-17

microsoft.com is Microsoft's product storefront and marketing front door — an Akamai-cached portal whose deep corpus actually lives on sibling docs, support, and store hosts.

 apple.com 2026-06-16

apple.com is a freely crawlable marketing shell wrapping Apple's real index asset: a no-auth media-catalog API for apps, music, and podcasts — rate-capped, not walled.

 translate.google.com 2026-06-15

Google Translate is a function, not a corpus: 243 languages translated on demand, nothing archived — the only bulk path is a paid per-character API.

 pinterest.com 2026-06-14

Pinterest is a 631M-user visual discovery engine whose pin corpus is reachable only through a 315-bot crawl allowlist, per-URL oEmbed, and an account-scoped API.

 imdb.com 2026-06-13

IMDb is the web's entertainment graph: 12.6M titles and 15.4M names ship as free daily dumps you may not legally turn into a searchable index.

 fandom.com 2026-06-12

Fandom hosts 250K CC-licensed fan wikis whose HTML is Cloudflare-walled but whose MediaWiki API, XML dumps, and image CDN remain wide open.

 linkedin.com 2026-06-11

LinkedIn is the professional-graph monopoly: 1.3B member profiles Microsoft monetizes at $19B/year, crawlable only by whitelisted search engines and litigated against everyone else.

 amazon.com 2026-06-10

Amazon is the web's default product index: a $717B store whose ASIN catalog, offers, and reviews are metered out through sales-gated APIs, not crawling.

 tiktok.com 2026-06-09

TikTok is a billion-user short-video network: 19-digit video IDs, page-embedded JSON state, keyless oEmbed, and a researcher-gated 100K-records/day Research API.

 instagram.com 2026-06-08

Instagram is Meta's 3B-user visual network: billions of images and Reels behind a login wall, no public content API, and a researcher-only bulk path.

 google.com 2026-06-07

Google is the web's index of record — 5T+ queries a year, ~90% share — yet offers no legitimate external path to its own SERPs.

 reddit.com 2026-06-06

Reddit is the web's lived-experience corpus: 25B+ posts and comments behind a default-deny robots.txt, sold to AI companies as a licensing revenue line.

 facebook.com 2026-06-05

Facebook is Meta's 3-billion-user social graph, now nearly sealed: default-deny robots.txt, login walls, app-review APIs, and a researcher-gated Content Library replacing CrowdTangle.

 wikipedia.org 2026-06-04

Wikipedia is the web's openest major corpus: 65M+ CC BY-SA articles in 300+ languages, with full dumps, free APIs, and a live edit stream.

 youtube.com 2026-06-03

YouTube is the web's largest video corpus: 20B+ videos, 20M added daily, crawlable page by page but with no enumeration path and gated transcripts.
