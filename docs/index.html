Daily Site Profiles

A mobile-friendly archive for deep profiles of high-traffic websites.

Open this page from GitHub Pages on a phone to read the reports directly.

microsoft.com 2026-06-17

microsoft.com is Microsoft's product storefront and marketing front door — an Akamai-cached portal whose deep corpus actually lives on sibling docs, support, and store hosts.

 apple.com 2026-06-16

apple.com is a freely crawlable marketing shell wrapping Apple's real index asset: a no-auth media-catalog API for apps, music, and podcasts — rate-capped, not walled.

 translate.google.com 2026-06-15

Google Translate is a function, not a corpus: 243 languages translated on demand, nothing archived — the only bulk path is a paid per-character API.

 pinterest.com 2026-06-14

Pinterest is a 631M-user visual discovery engine whose pin corpus is reachable only through a 315-bot crawl allowlist, per-URL oEmbed, and an account-scoped API.

 imdb.com 2026-06-13

IMDb is the web's entertainment graph: 12.6M titles and 15.4M names ship as free daily dumps you may not legally turn into a searchable index.

 fandom.com 2026-06-12

Fandom hosts 250K CC-licensed fan wikis whose HTML is Cloudflare-walled but whose MediaWiki API, XML dumps, and image CDN remain wide open.

 linkedin.com 2026-06-11

LinkedIn is the professional-graph monopoly: 1.3B member profiles Microsoft monetizes at $19B/year, crawlable only by whitelisted search engines and litigated against everyone else.

 amazon.com 2026-06-10

Amazon is the web's default product index: a $717B store whose ASIN catalog, offers, and reviews are metered out through sales-gated APIs, not crawling.

 tiktok.com 2026-06-09

TikTok is a billion-user short-video network: 19-digit video IDs, page-embedded JSON state, keyless oEmbed, and a researcher-gated 100K-records/day Research API.

 instagram.com 2026-06-08

Instagram is Meta's 3B-user visual network: billions of images and Reels behind a login wall, no public content API, and a researcher-only bulk path.

 google.com 2026-06-07

Google is the web's index of record — 5T+ queries a year, ~90% share — yet offers no legitimate external path to its own SERPs.

 reddit.com 2026-06-06

Reddit is the web's lived-experience corpus: 25B+ posts and comments behind a default-deny robots.txt, sold to AI companies as a licensing revenue line.

 facebook.com 2026-06-05

Facebook is Meta's 3-billion-user social graph, now nearly sealed: default-deny robots.txt, login walls, app-review APIs, and a researcher-gated Content Library replacing CrowdTangle.

 wikipedia.org 2026-06-04

Wikipedia is the web's openest major corpus: 65M+ CC BY-SA articles in 300+ languages, with full dumps, free APIs, and a live edit stream.

 youtube.com 2026-06-03

YouTube is the web's largest video corpus: 20B+ videos, 20M added daily, crawlable page by page but with no enumeration path and gated transcripts.
