Context

xHamster is one of the world's largest pornographic tube sites: free, ad-supported streaming of user-uploaded and studio video, plus photo galleries, erotic stories, creator profiles, and an embedded live-cam surface. It launched in August 2007 and is operated by Hammy Media Ltd, registered in Limassol, Cyprus; founders Oleg Netepenko and Dmitri Gusev have kept a low profile. Unlike Pornhub/RedTube/YouPorn (Aylo) or the WGCZ family (XVideos), xHamster sits in its own ownership cluster. Money comes from display/video advertising, a premium subscription tier, and revenue-share on cams and creator content. For a full-modal index it matters as a very large, loosely-licensed media corpus that is age-gated, geo-blocked in several countries, and — notably — declines to publish EU recipient counts, asserting it falls below the 45M threshold that designated its three largest rivals as DSA Very Large Online Platforms.

Retrieval Notes (site-specific deltas)

Only where xhamster.com differs from the default platform assumptions.

Identifier & dedup

Canonical key is the trailing video ID, not the slug — slugs are SEO-mutable. Two ID schemes coexist (legacy numeric, newer xh-base62); index on the ID, treat the slug as a display alias. Same clip reposts across channels/mirrors are common, so dedup on perceptual video hash, not URL.

Authoritative content field

No JSON-LD; OpenGraph is the structured truth. When title, og:description (Starring/Channel/Category), and free tags disagree, the OG description is the cleanest operator-curated field; the long tag list is SEO-inflated and noisy.

Cross-modal joins

Video ID joins to comments, thumbnail set, pornstar/creator/channel entities, and category/tag nodes. Pornstar and channel IDs are stable join keys; free-text tags are not — normalize them to the 655 canonical categories.

Quality & spam

robots.txt suppresses precisely the spam-magnet surfaces — duration/fps/geo facets, most-viewed/most-commented sorts, deep pagination. Trust the allow-listed best views over raw sort facets; treat tag clouds as low-trust.

Freshness & recrawl

Counts (views/likes) mutate fastest; titles/tags slowly; the clip body is immutable until deletion. Deletions follow DMCA and country-block waves, so expect bursty 404s rather than steady decay.

Access posture

Unusually crawler-open for an adult site: no AI-bot blocks and no sitemap. Cloudflare rate-limits bursts to 520 rather than serving a sitemap, so ingestion is paced page-crawl, not bulk pull.

Indexing Plan

How Site Wise would build the index for this site.

  • Retrieval unit: the video, keyed by trailing ID, with title, OG description, category/tag set, pornstar/channel/creator entities, comment counts, and a thumbnail as attached fields.
  • Primary keys & joins: video ID ↔ pornstar ID ↔ channel ID ↔ canonical category node; perceptual hash for cross-repost dedup.
  • Ingestion paths, ranked: 1) polite allow-listed page crawl of /videos, /categories, /pornstars, /creators (no auth; ceiling = robots allow-list + Cloudflare rate limits, no sitemap so discovery is link-graph-bound); 2) category/best-view pagination 2–10 for breadth; 3) OG scrape per page for clean metadata. No API, no firehose, no bulk dump.
  • Recrawl cadence: hot/new videos daily for count drift; back catalog monthly; entity/category pages weekly; expect deletion bursts on legal events.
  • Derivatives to generate: ASR transcripts and keyframe/thumbnail embeddings for video search; tag-to-category normalization; perceptual hashes for dedup. Skip OCR (little on-screen text value).
  • Demo to build: a category-census crawler that snapshots the 655 category nodes and their top "best" videos, producing an entity graph (category ↔ pornstar ↔ channel) with no auth and no media mirroring.

Risks to the Index

Only risks that change indexing strategy.

  • DSA reclassification: if the EU forces VLOP designation (as it did Pornhub/Stripchat/XVideos in Dec 2023 and XNXX in Jul 2024), expect a real sitemap/transparency surface to appear — but also harder age-gating that can break anonymous crawl.
  • Age-verification walls: US state laws (the 2025 Texas settlement) and UK rules push toward identity gates that could move the whole catalog behind auth in some regions, shrinking the anonymous indexable surface.
  • Country blocks & mirror rotation: bans (India, Russia, Germany, Nepal historically) spawn rotating mirror domains; index on content hash + stable video ID so mirror churn doesn't fragment the corpus.
  • Licensing ambiguity: mixed UGC/studio rights with no bulk license — safe to index metadata and hotlink thumbnails, unsafe to mirror media.
