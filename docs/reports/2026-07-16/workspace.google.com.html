Context

workspace.google.com is Google's B2B storefront and self-serve sign-up funnel for Google Workspace, the subscription bundle wrapping Gmail, Drive, Docs, Sheets, and Meet for organizations. Google LLC (Alphabet Inc., NASDAQ: GOOGL) owns it; the product launched Aug 28 2006 as Google Apps for Your Domain, was renamed G Suite in Sep 2016, then Google Workspace on Oct 6 2020. Revenue is per-seat subscription across self-serve Business tiers plus custom-priced Enterprise; Google bundled Gemini AI into every paid tier and raised list prices 17-22% on Jan 15 2025. For an index builder, this host is not the Workspace product itself — Gmail, Drive, Docs, and Meet each run on their own separate host, out of scope here — but a marketing funnel: product-tour pages, industry landing pages, 326 customer case studies, and pricing. inferred The whole surface is IP-geolocated rather than negotiated by request headers. Retrieval Notes (site-specific deltas)

Only where this site differs from the default platform assumptions.

Identifier & dedup

The only variation is the /intl/{locale}/ path prefix (and equivalent ?hl= query variants). Strip both to reach the canonical path; 8,789 sitemap entries collapse to 773 canonical pages (Probe Log).

Retrieval unit

The page itself — there is no sub-page entity to key on except the 326 customer case studies, whose natural key is the URL slug (customer name).

Cross-modal joins

Case-study slug joins the page copy to the customer's logo image. Nothing else joins across modality; no external ID (G2, Crunchbase, ticker) was observed in sampled markup.

Authoritative content field

Single-author corporate copy, so no third-party SEO distortion. The distortion risk instead sits in the price block: the "50% off" promotional figure observed 2026-07-16 is geo- and session-dependent, not a stable list price (Probe Log).

Freshness & recrawl

Two speeds on one page: the pricing/promo banner runs on a marketing calendar while surrounding product copy is evergreen for months. One recrawl cadence for the whole page either wastes cycles on the static majority or misses the promo delta.

Quality & spam

None — no UGC on this host; customer testimonials are Google-selected and Google-published.

Internal vs external search

No on-site search box was observed on the pages sampled; the funnel routes through direct navigation and Google Search results rather than an internal index.

User query profile

Comparison/evaluation intent ("Google Workspace vs Microsoft 365"), plan-and-price lookups, and industry-specific case-study queries — commercial research queries, not conversational ones.

Indexing Plan

  • Retrieval unit: canonical (non-/intl/) page, keyed by URL path.
  • Primary keys & joins: URL path stripped of /intl/{locale}/ and ?hl=; customer-story slug joins page copy to logo image.
  • Ingestion paths, ranked: 1) Sitemap-driven fetch of the ~447 non-customer, non-Marketplace canonical pages — fully allowed by robots.txt. 2) The 326 customer case studies and the Marketplace catalog are sitemap-listed but robots.txt-disallowed for every UA; the only respectful path is a general search engine's own cached snippet, not a first-party crawl. 3) YouTube Data API for embedded product-tour videos, keyed by the video ID scraped from allowed pages.
  • Recrawl cadence: pricing/plan page weekly (promo churn); product/solution/industry pages monthly; new customer-story or Marketplace-app slugs tracked via sitemap diff only, without fetching the disallowed page itself.
  • Derivatives to generate: de-duplication embeddings to collapse the 11.4x locale duplication to one canonical record per page; none needed beyond that for evergreen marketing copy.
  • Demo to build: a sitemap-diff watcher — fetch the 10 child sitemaps, strip /intl/ variants, and alert on new /customers/ or /marketplace/ slugs without ever fetching the disallowed paths themselves, demonstrating a "new case study detector" that respects robots.txt.

Risks to the Index

  • Sitemap/robots contradiction: robots.txt blocks /customers and /marketplace/ for every UA while the sitemap actively lists both — the site's richest content (326 case studies, the app catalog) has no respectful bulk-crawl path at all (Probe Log).
  • Pricing is a single-vantage-point trap: the price shown at any given fetch is geo- and promo-dependent (PLN, 50% off, observed 2026-07-16); a crawl from one location bakes in one country's promotional price as if it were the global list price.
  • inferred No stated stance on AI-agent crawling. Basis: robots.txt (9 lines, observed 2026-07-16) carries no named AI-crawler rules — unlike Amazon's ~30-agent blanket ban — and /llms.txt returns 404; absence of a rule is not the same as a durable allowance.
