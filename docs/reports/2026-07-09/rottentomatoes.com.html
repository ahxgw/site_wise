Context

Rotten Tomatoes aggregates professional critic reviews into a Tomatometer score and ticket/stream-verified viewer opinions into a Popcornmeter score, for movies, TV, and the people who make them. It does not review anything itself; it curates and counts. Revenue is display advertising plus affiliate ticketing and streaming links (Fandango at Home, part of the same corporate family) — no subscription paywall gates any score or review. As of Jan 2026 the site sits inside Versant Media Group, a Comcast/NBCUniversal spinoff, with Warner Bros. Discovery holding a minority stake in the shared parent, Fandango Media — the same three-studio-and-cable-conglomerate ownership churn that has passed through IGN, News Corp, and Flixster since 1998. For an index builder the object of interest is the title page (movie, TV series, season, or episode) and the two score blocks attached to it, plus a very large person/critic graph: sitemaps expose 2.3M+ celebrity and critic profile URLs versus roughly 400K movie and 1.2M TV-episode pages combined (Probe Log).

Retrieval Notes (site-specific deltas)

Only where this site differs from the default platform assumptions.

Identifier & dedup

URL slugs double as IDs (/m/{slug}, /tv/{slug}/s{N}/e{N}, /celebrity/{slug}, /critic/{slug}, /source-{numeric-id}) with no separate canonical numeric ID exposed on the page itself; the JSON-LD url field is the de facto canonical form.

Retrieval unit

Two tiers per title: the title page (synopsis, cast, one aggregate score pair) is fully server-rendered and crawlable; the reviews sub-page is not — individual review text sits behind client-side rendering this probe could not resolve to a concrete endpoint. Treat "title metadata + score" and "review corpus" as separate retrieval problems with very different feasibility.

Cross-modal joins

The /celebrity/ and /critic/ slug is the join key between a person and every title they appear on (via JSON-LD actor/director sameAs links back to the profile URL); the /source-* ID plays the same role for a publication across its critics' reviews, though the source↔critic mapping was not itself sampled in this pass.

Quality & spam

The Popcornmeter's "Verified" tier (Fandango-ticket or Fandango-at-Home-stream gated, per the embedded score block's scoreType:"VERIFIED") is RT's own defense against vote-brigading on the older ungated user-score system; an index treating audience score as a trust signal should prefer the verified variant over any legacy unverified score still visible in the UI.

Indexing Plan

The actionable conclusion: how Site Wise would build the index for this site.

  • Retrieval unit: the title page (movie, TV series, season, or episode) as the primary unit, carrying its own JSON-LD block and score summary; person/critic/publication pages as a secondary entity graph joined by slug.
  • Primary keys & joins: URL slug per object type; JSON-LD sameAs links join a title's cast/director list to celebrity profile URLs.
  • Ingestion paths, ranked: 1) Sitemap-listed URLs fetched by ordinary HTTP, no bot-wall observed (coverage: all 3.47M main-site + 21.8K editorial URLs). 2) Per-page JSON-LD plus the media-scorecard-json block for the Tomatometer/Popcornmeter pair (coverage: every title page, but score-only — no review text). 3) Business Proposal Form for a negotiated data feed if bulk review text or a lower-latency feed is required (coverage and cost: unknown, no self-serve terms published).
  • Recrawl cadence: title pages around new-release and post-release review windows (scores move fastest in the first weeks); person/critic pages on a slow, filmography-driven cadence; the sitemap's uniform lastmod cannot drive this — cadence must be inferred from each object's own release calendar instead.
  • Derivatives to generate: none justified from this pass — no bulk image or video corpus was confirmed reachable beyond one hotlinked poster/trailer per title, and review text (the modality that would justify sentiment/embedding work) is not fetchable without resolving the client-side reviews endpoint.
  • Demo to build: a headless-browser trace of /m/*/reviews to identify the actual client-side request that populates review text, turning the current Probe Log "open" into a documented endpoint or a confirmed no-legitimate-path finding.

Risks to the Index

Only risks that change indexing strategy.

  • Review text retrieval depends on reverse-engineering an undocumented client-side endpoint rather than a stable, published API; that endpoint can change without notice since it is not a contracted surface.
  • Ownership just changed hands (Versant spinoff completed Jan 2 2026, Verified Metrics) — a newly independent public company may revisit data-licensing terms (the Business Proposal Form path observed in the Probe Log) faster than a stable incumbent would.
