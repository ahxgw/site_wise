Context

LinkedIn is Microsoft's professional network and the de facto registry of working identity: who works where, on what, since when. Revenue comes from four lines — Talent Solutions (recruiting), Marketing Solutions (B2B ads), Premium Subscriptions, and Sales Solutions — all of which are paid views into the same member graph. For an index builder, the prize is the entity graph (person ↔ company ↔ job ↔ skill ↔ school), and LinkedIn runs the purest two-track access model of any major platform: robots.txt defaults every agent to Disallow: / and tells would-be crawlers to email whitelist-crawl@linkedin.com; the ~42 approved search crawlers get a curated guest-page sliver; everyone else gets HTTP 999 and, if persistent, a lawsuit. inferred The guest sliver is deliberately rich, not residual. Basis: guest pages serve full schema.org JSON-LD — Person with employment dates and follower counts, Organization with measured headcount, JobPosting with expiry — exactly the fields a search engine needs to build rich results (Probe Log, 2026-06-11).

Retrieval Notes (site-specific deltas)

Only where LinkedIn differs from the default platform assumptions.

Identifier & dedup

Public URLs use mutable vanity slugs (/in/satyanadella, /company/microsoft) — members can change them, so the visible key is unstable; internal numeric member IDs never appear on guest pages. Job IDs are stable 10-digit numerics in /jobs/view/{id}. An external index must treat the person-entity, not the URL, as primary and survive slug rotation.

Retrieval unit

Three units with different lifetimes: the person entity (durable, slow churn), the job posting (born with an expiry date), and the post (fast decay, only transiently visible to guests). A single recrawl policy across them fails on all three.

Cross-modal joins

Guest JSON-LD hands over the joins directly: Person.worksFor links to the company URL with an employment startDate; JobPosting.hiringOrganization links the same way; skills strings map to the 42K-term taxonomy. The graph edges are in the markup — rare among large platforms.

Authoritative content field

Everything person-side is self-reported — titles, dates, skills are claims, not records. The company-side check: numberOfEmployees in Organization JSON-LD read 232,349 for Microsoft (Probe Log) vs ~228,000 in Microsoft's own FY2025 10-K — LinkedIn's figure counts affiliated profiles, not payroll. Treat platform-measured aggregates and member claims as separate trust tiers.

Internal vs external search

People search is login-gated and /search* is disallowed even for Googlebot (Probe Log), so Google's copy of the guest sliver is the only public people-search over LinkedIn — site:linkedin.com/in queries are a load-bearing public utility the platform itself doesn't offer.

User query profile

Navigational person lookup (name + company), recruiter sourcing, job search, and B2B prospecting. Almost every external query is an entity query, which is why the JSON-LD sliver matters more than page text.

Indexing Plan

  • Retrieval unit: person entity assembled from guest-page JSON-LD, with job postings and company entities as separate corpora; posts indexed opportunistically at profile-crawl time, never recrawled.
  • Primary keys & joins: person = canonical profile URL with slug-rotation history; company = slug + Organization URL; job = numeric ID. Join people↔companies via worksFor, jobs↔companies via hiringOrganization.
  • Ingestion paths, ranked: 1) Whitelist application (whitelist-crawl@linkedin.com) for guest pages — the only path to profiles; coverage ceiling is the public-profile sliver. 2) jobs-guest endpoint + JobPosting JSON-LD — no auth observed, 30/page; the jobs corpus is effectively open. 3) Partner APIs — own-data scoped (your ads, your hires); useless for a general index. 4) Unauthorized crawl — ruled out: HTTP 999 at the edge and the hiQ injunction at the courthouse.
  • Recrawl cadence: jobs daily until validThrough; profiles event-driven (role-change signals) with a monthly floor; company pages weekly; skills taxonomy quarterly.
  • Derivatives to generate: employment-timeline normalization across profiles, company-alias resolution (slug history + name variants), skill-taxonomy mapping, name-disambiguation embeddings for the people index.
  • Demo to build: a jobs-guest pagination harvester that sweeps keyword × region cells, dedups job IDs, and estimates the live listings count — closing this report's first open question with a measurement.

Risks to the Index

  • Enforcement is judicial, not just technical: hiQ ended with a $500K judgment, a permanent injunction, and court-ordered destruction of derived algorithms (Verified Metrics). LinkedIn is the platform most likely to sue an indexer; the whitelist application is also a contract surface.
  • The guest sliver is unilateral and unversioned: no sitemap, no published guarantee — robots.txt curates per-UA and changed silently before. Today's 200s (Probe Log) are an observation, not an API contract; directory pages already return 999.
  • inferred AI access is heading toward selective deals, not open licensing. Basis: robots.txt admits OAI-SearchBot to the approved-crawler track while banning Claude-SearchBot, PerplexityBot, and 30+ other AI agents outright (Probe Log, 2026-06-11) — a per-company allowlist, not a policy category.
  • The jobs-guest endpoint is undocumented: the one no-auth structured feed has no stated terms or rate ceiling and can close without notice; the jobs index needs a fallback via JobPosting markup on the whitelist track.

Inferences

  • The guest-page sliver is deliberately engineered for search engines rather than a residual public surface. Basis: rich Person/Organization/JobPosting JSON-LD on guest pages combined with per-UA robots curation observed 2026-06-11.
  • LinkedIn enforces by user-agent gating plus contract litigation rather than relying on robots.txt compliance. Basis: HTTP 999 for non-browser UAs (Probe Log) and the hiQ judgment resting on breach of contract, not CFAA.
  • Organization numberOfEmployees is a platform-measured profile count, not company-reported headcount. Basis: 232,349 for Microsoft in guest JSON-LD vs ~228,000 in Microsoft's FY2025 10-K.
  • AI access will be granted per-company, not per-category. Basis: OAI-SearchBot on the approved-crawler list while Claude-SearchBot and PerplexityBot sit in the blanket-ban list (robots.txt, 2026-06-11).
